Caffeine!

Across centuries and continents, humans have turned to caffeine, in tea, coffee, and cacao, for energy, ritual, and comfort. But how did this small molecule come to dominate cultures worldwide? To understand caffeine’s hold on modern life, we first look back. From chemistry labs to coffee houses, here’s how caffeine evolved into a global phenomenon.

Evolution of Caffeine — Story Timeline

As coffee spread across empires and oceans, it became more than a drink: it became a trade network. Today, much of the world’s caffeine supply traces back to a narrow equatorial region known as the Coffee Belt.
